The align-content property works on the cross axis. … By adding the align-content property with a value of space-between, the items take as much space as they need for their content; then, the remaining space is distributed between the items. This works in the same way as justify-content on the main axis.

Adding the align-content and justify-content properties with a value of space-between spaces out the tracks in both dimensions.

FIG 4.14 and 4.15 illustrate that when we use space-between, not only do our grid gaps appear to get wider, but so do any grid areas that span more than one track. Thatâ€™s because they also span over gaps, so they need to include the extra space. This can result in a fairly dramatic size increase, something to keep in mind if your design makes use of these properties.
