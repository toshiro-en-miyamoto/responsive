Elements that are in flow appear per their formatting model.

  • The formatting model for block-level elements means they take the full width of the container (if not otherwise restricted) and appear on a new line.
  • Inline elements display next to each other if there is room — just like words in a sentence.

If we float an element, we take it out of flow. For floated items, this means the item moves up until it encounters a block-level element; following elements then move up alongside the floated item.
